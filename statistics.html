New York COVID-19 Vaccine Statistics

18.5M

As of March 2025, over 18.5 million vaccine doses have been administered in New York State, with 15.8 million individuals receiving at least one dose of the COVID-19 vaccine. This represents approximately 95% of the state's total population.

Fully Vaccinated Individuals

Approximately 15.8 million New Yorkers are fully vaccinated, representing 81% of the population. This includes people who have completed the required number of doses for the initial vaccine series.

Booster Shots Administered

More than 5.3 million booster shots have been administered to New Yorkers, reaching about 30% of the population. Booster shot uptake varies by age, with older adults being the primary recipients of boosters.

Vaccination Breakdown by Gender

Women have been more likely to get vaccinated compared to men. Approximately 72% of women in New York have received at least one dose, while 66% of men have been vaccinated. This trend is consistent across different age groups and ethnicities.

Age Group Vaccination Rates

The vaccination rates vary significantly by age group:

  • Adults aged 65 and older: Over 85% of people in this age group are fully vaccinated, making it the highest vaccinated demographic in the state.
  • Adults aged 45-64: Approximately 80% of this group has received at least one dose.
  • Adults aged 25-44: About 70% have been vaccinated, with many in this age group opting for the vaccine later than other groups.
  • Young adults aged 18-24: Vaccination rates in this group are around 65%, with a noticeable decline in uptake compared to older age groups.
  • Teens aged 12-17: Around 75% of teenagers have received at least one dose of the vaccine, as schools and universities have played a significant role in encouraging vaccination.

Vaccination Rates by Ethnicity

Vaccination rates in New York vary by ethnicity, with some groups having lower rates than others:

  • White New Yorkers: Approximately 85% have received at least one dose, making this group the most vaccinated.
  • Asian New Yorkers: Vaccination rates are also high, at about 80%, though slightly lower than that of White residents.
  • Black New Yorkers: Around 65% of Black New Yorkers have been vaccinated, with targeted outreach efforts aimed at increasing this rate.
  • Hispanic/Latino New Yorkers: Approximately 68% of this community has been vaccinated, with significant efforts to improve vaccine access and information in Spanish-speaking communities.
  • Other Ethnic Groups: Vaccination rates in Native American and other ethnic minority groups are around 60%.

Vaccination Rates by Region

There are noticeable regional differences in vaccination rates across New York:

  • New York City: Vaccination rates in NYC are among the highest in the state, with 80% of the population receiving at least one dose. This is due to the city's concentrated efforts in vaccine distribution, especially in diverse neighborhoods.
  • Long Island: Vaccination rates on Long Island are slightly lower than in NYC, with approximately 75% of the population vaccinated.
  • Upstate New York: Rates in upstate New York are more variable, with urban areas like Albany and Buffalo showing higher vaccination rates (70-80%), while rural areas have significantly lower rates (50-60%).

Vaccination Among Healthcare Workers

Healthcare workers in New York have generally had high vaccination uptake, with approximately 95% of medical professionals receiving at least one dose. Efforts to ensure the safety of healthcare workers have been critical in ensuring high vaccination rates within this group.

Vaccination Rates Among Students

Vaccination rates for students have seen a significant increase, especially with mandates in place for universities and K-12 schools. Over 80% of college students in New York have been vaccinated, while 70% of high school students have received at least one dose. The state continues to encourage vaccination through school programs and public health campaigns.

Vaccination by Employment Sector

The vaccination rates also vary by employment sector. Key sectors include:

  • Education: Approximately 75% of educators have received the vaccine.
  • Retail and Hospitality: These sectors have lower vaccination rates, around 60-65%, with ongoing efforts to provide easy access to vaccines for essential workers.
  • Public Transportation: Approximately 70% of public transit workers are vaccinated.

Challenges and Future Vaccination Plans

New York has focused efforts on increasing vaccination rates in certain populations that remain hesitant or underserved. These efforts include:

  • Mobile vaccination units for rural areas and underserved neighborhoods.
  • Community outreach programs to address vaccine hesitancy in specific populations, particularly Black and Latino communities.
  • Targeted campaigns to increase booster shot uptake, particularly in older adults and people with underlying health conditions.
