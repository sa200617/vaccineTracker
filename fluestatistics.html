New York Flu Vaccination Statistics

10.2M

As of 2025, more than 10.2 million flu vaccine doses have been administered in New York State, with 9.5 million individuals receiving the flu vaccine. This represents about 88% of the state's total population during the flu season.

Fully Vaccinated Individuals

Approximately 9.5 million New Yorkers are fully vaccinated against the flu, representing 85% of the population. This includes people who have received the annual flu shot during the flu season.

Flu Vaccine Uptake by Age Group

Flu vaccination uptake varies across different age groups:

  • Adults aged 65 and older: 80% of this age group has received the flu vaccine.
  • Adults aged 45-64: Approximately 75% of individuals in this group have been vaccinated.
  • Adults aged 25-44: About 70% have received the vaccine during the flu season.
  • Adults aged 18-24: Flu vaccine uptake in this group is around 60%.
  • Children aged 6-17: Approximately 70% of children have been vaccinated against the flu this season.

Flu Vaccination Rates by Ethnicity

The flu vaccination rates in New York vary by ethnicity:

  • White New Yorkers: Approximately 85% have received the flu vaccine.
  • Asian New Yorkers: About 80% have been vaccinated against the flu.
  • Black New Yorkers: Around 65% of Black New Yorkers have received the flu vaccine.
  • Hispanic/Latino New Yorkers: Vaccination rates are about 68% among the Latino population.
  • Other Ethnic Groups: Flu vaccination rates are approximately 60% for other ethnic groups.

Flu Vaccination Rates by Region

Flu vaccination rates differ significantly across regions in New York:

  • New York City: Vaccination rates in NYC are high, at approximately 75% of the population.
  • Long Island: About 70% of Long Island residents have been vaccinated against the flu.
  • Upstate New York: Vaccination rates in upstate New York vary, with urban areas having rates of 70-80%, while rural areas lag behind with only 50-60% vaccinated.

Flu Vaccination Rates Among Healthcare Workers

Healthcare workers have a high flu vaccination rate, with about 95% of New York healthcare professionals receiving their flu vaccine each year.

Flu Vaccination Rates Among Students

Student vaccination rates for the flu have also been strong, with approximately 80% of college students vaccinated, and 70% of high school students receiving the flu shot.

Challenges and Future Flu Vaccination Plans

New York has faced challenges with flu vaccination uptake in certain demographics, including:

  • Efforts to target young adults who are less likely to receive the flu vaccine.
  • Outreach programs in underserved communities to increase vaccine access and awareness.
  • Increasing flu vaccine uptake in rural areas through mobile vaccination clinics and educational campaigns.
